Case Study 01

Testing Financial Systems

A decade spent inside the financial systems that include mobile wallets, PSP integrations, lending platforms, and corporate card transactions, across markets in Africa and Europe.

Companies Cellulant, JUMO, Pleo, Sketch
Domain Mobile money, lending, savings, corporate cards
Tools Postman, SQL, Playwright, Cypress, Grafana

The Problem Space

Over the course of my QA career, I've spent most of my time working on systems where a small defect can have real financial consequences. These include digital banking platforms, payment gateways, mobile wallets, lending and savings products, insurance, corporate card platforms, and financial reconciliation services used across Africa and Europe. Unlike many applications where an issue might cause an inconvenience for a user, financial platforms require every calculation, transaction, and integration to behave exactly as expected. My role has always been to understand how money moves through a system, identify edge cases before they reach production, and verify that every transaction is processed correctly from start to finish.

What I Tested

  • Multi-market, multi-currency payment flows and reconciliation logic at Cellulant, including PSP integrations and the Bank of Agriculture digital banking platform in Nigeria.
  • Loan, savings, and insurance calculation logic, eligibility rules, and payout flows at JUMO, where data integrity had direct financial consequences for users across Africa and Europe.
  • Card transaction logic and multi-user expense categorization at Pleo, an EU fintech unicorn.

Some of the systems I've tested

At Cellulant, I tested multi market and multi currency payment platforms, PSP integrations, merchant payment flows, and reconciliation processes. I also worked on the Bank of Agriculture's digital banking platform in Nigeria, validating transaction flows and ensuring banking operations behaved correctly across different services. At JUMO, I tested lending, savings, insurance, and customer eligibility logic. This involved verifying financial calculations, repayment schedules, product rules, payout processes, and data consistency across services supporting millions of customers in Africa and Europe. At Pleo, I worked on corporate card payments, transaction processing, expense categorisation, and collaboration features that allow multiple users to manage company spending accurately. Alongside fintech, I've also tested SaaS platforms and design products, giving me experience with very different user journeys while applying the same disciplined approach to quality, reliability, and customer experience.

My approach to testing

Most of my work has involved much more than validating the user interface. I regularly use Postman to test APIs directly, SQL to verify data in the database, and observability tools such as Grafana to investigate production behaviour and monitor distributed systems. Rather than assuming a transaction completed because the interface says it did, I trace requests through the services involved and confirm that the underlying data matches the expected outcome. Working closely with developers, product managers, and business teams has also taught me the importance of understanding the business rules behind a feature, not just the acceptance criteria.

What this experience has taught mea

Testing financial systems has reinforced the value of precision, curiosity, and attention to detail. Whether I am validating a payment, a lending decision, a reconciliation process, or a hospitality booking system, I approach testing in the same way. I want to understand how the system works, where it can fail, and whether it continues to behave correctly under real world conditions. As software continues to evolve, I have also been investing time in understanding AI assisted testing and the unique challenges that AI powered applications introduce. While the technology is changing quickly, the fundamentals remain the same. Good testing still depends on asking the right questions, validating outcomes, understanding risk, and building confidence that a system behaves as intended. The tools may change, but a thoughtful testing mindset continues to be the most valuable skill I can bring to any product.
